## Product information
Overview  Eligibility & Conditions  Application Guide 
**The Project Home Loan package** at MSB is a flexible financing solution designed for customers looking to own homes in real estate development projects. Partnering with leading developers in the market such as Vingroup, Sun Group, and Novaland, MSB offers tailored financing options aligned with the characteristics of each project. With a high loan limit, long loan tenure, and flexible principal grace periods, customers can better manage their cash flow and reduce financial pressure during the early stages of homeownership. Combined with a fast approval process and dedicated advisory support throughout the journey, MSB helps customers move forward with confidence on their path to investment and homeownership.
  * Aged from 22 to 70, and not exceeding 80 years old at loan maturity (calculated by year).
  * Minimum monthly income of VND 8 million.
  * Collateral required: The property to be purchased or other eligible assets may be used as collateral at MSB.


**Step 1: Loan Application Registration**
An MSB relationship manager will contact you within 24 hours after receiving your application.
**Step 2: Document Submission**
  * ID Card/Citizen Identification Card and Household Registration Book (copies).
  * Collateral ownership certificate (copy).
  * Proof of income documents.
  * Sale and Purchase/Transfer Agreement
  * Payment schedule notice issued by the Developer.
  * Deposit payment receipt.


**Step 3: Signing of Credit and Security Agreements**
Including notarization, certification, and registration of collateral security.
**Step 4: Loan Disbursement by MSB**
Funds will be disbursed immediately after completion of collateral security registration procedures

## Frequently Asked Questions
[ Learn More ](https://www.msb.com.vn/en/contact-us-support/faqs/#retail-banking)
Am I exempt from interest payments during the interest rate subsidy period?
15/03/2026
In practice, the loan continues to accrue interest during the interest support period; however, the developer pays this interest to the Bank on behalf of the customer. The customer only needs to repay the loan principal and any late payment interest or penalties (if the principal is not repaid on time).
What is the required down payment for purchasing an apartment or a landed project?
15/03/2026
Depending on each specific project, MSB may require different levels of customer equity, but in all cases it will not exceed 30% of the property sale and purchase contract value.
What are Partnered Projects?
15/03/2026
These are projects that have been appraised, approved for partnership, and have signed cooperation agreements with MSB. Customers purchasing homes from projects partnered with MSB may enjoy more preferential loan policies compared to loans from other banks.
I am acquiring a property that has not yet been issued a certificate of ownership; can I mortgage this asset for a loan at MSB?
15/03/2026
Yes. MSB supports financing for customers purchasing project-based real estate (partnered with MSB) that has not yet been issued a certificate, or properties that already have a certificate but have not completed the ownership transfer procedures. In such cases, the collateral is considered an asset formed from the loan proceeds.
What is a disbursement that places the funds under hold?
15/03/2026
This is a financing method whereby MSB transfers the loan amount to the seller’s account or the account of a person authorized by the seller and places the funds under hold. The funds will be released once the sale and purchase contract is notarized/certified or once the ownership transfer of the property certificate has been completed (depending on the specific case).
Can I repay the loan principal before maturity, and if so, will I be charged a prepayment fee?
15/03/2026
Yes. Customers may repay part or all of the loan principal before maturity depending on their financial situation. Early repayment fees are calculated based on the prepaid principal amount; however, most MSB interest rate packages waive early repayment fees from the fifth year onward.
